# Lab 3: Risk Management Planning

## Overview
Developed a formal risk management plan for the healthcare organization scenario. Defined the scope, objectives, roles and responsibilities, and the overall risk management lifecycle to be applied across the organization's critical systems (HNetExchange, HNetPay, Payroll, and Accounting).

## Skills Demonstrated
- Designing a risk management plan aligned to industry frameworks
- Defining risk appetite, tolerance thresholds, and acceptance criteria
- Assigning roles and responsibilities within a risk governance structure
- Mapping the risk management lifecycle: Identify → Assess → Respond → Monitor

## Tools Used
- SimpleRisk (risk management documentation platform)
- NIST SP 800-30 Rev. 1 and NIST CSF reference documentation
- Risk plan templates

## Key Takeaways
- A well-defined risk management plan provides a consistent, repeatable process for identifying and treating risks
- Risk appetite and tolerance must be explicitly defined before risks can be prioritized effectively
- Executive sponsorship and clear ownership are critical success factors for any risk management programme
- Continuous monitoring is as important as initial risk identification

## Relevant Frameworks
- NIST SP 800-30 Rev. 1 — Guide for Conducting Risk Assessments
- NIST SP 800-39 — Managing Information Security Risk (Organization, Mission, and Information System View)
- NIST CSF — Identify (ID.RM): Risk Management Strategy
- ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — Clause 6.1: Actions to address risks and opportunities
