# Lab 5: IT Asset Inventory

## Overview
Created a comprehensive IT asset inventory for the healthcare organization scenario. Catalogued hardware, software, data assets, and network components across the critical systems (HNetExchange, HNetPay, Payroll, and Accounting), assigned criticality ratings, and identified asset owners responsible for each asset's security and availability.

## Skills Demonstrated
- Identifying and classifying IT assets by type (hardware, software, data, network)
- Assigning asset criticality ratings based on business impact
- Documenting asset ownership and custodianship
- Mapping assets to the systems and business processes they support

## Tools Used
- Nmap (network host and service discovery)
- nbtscan (NetBIOS device enumeration)
- Asset inventory spreadsheet / SimpleRisk
- NIST SP 800-30 asset identification guidance

## Key Takeaways
- A complete and accurate asset inventory is the foundation of any effective risk management or vulnerability management programme
- Assets must be classified by criticality to enable risk-based prioritization of security controls
- Unmanaged or undiscovered assets (shadow IT) represent a significant security risk that can only be addressed through active discovery
- Asset ownership assignment ensures accountability for patching, configuration management, and incident response

## Relevant Frameworks
- NIST CSF — Identify (ID.AM): Asset Management
- NIST SP 800-30 Rev. 1 — Asset identification as part of risk framing
- ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — Annex A 5.9: Inventory of information and other associated assets
- HIPAA Security Rule — Addressable implementation specification for hardware and software inventory
