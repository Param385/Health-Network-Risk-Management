# Lab 8: Business Impact Analysis

## Overview
Conducted a Business Impact Analysis (BIA) for the healthcare organization's critical business functions and supporting IT systems. Identified recovery time objectives (RTO), recovery point objectives (RPO), and maximum tolerable periods of disruption (MTPD) for each critical system, and mapped dependencies to inform business continuity and disaster recovery planning.

## Skills Demonstrated
- Identifying critical business functions and their IT system dependencies
- Defining RTO, RPO, and MTPD for each critical function and system
- Quantifying the financial, operational, regulatory, and reputational impacts of system disruption
- Producing a BIA report to inform business continuity and disaster recovery planning

## Tools Used
- BIA questionnaire and interview templates
- Business continuity planning (BCP) documentation frameworks
- SimpleRisk (impact documentation)
- Dependency mapping worksheets

## Key Takeaways
- RTO and RPO must be driven by business requirements, not solely by technical capabilities — IT recovery capabilities must be aligned to business needs
- MTPD defines the outer boundary beyond which an organization cannot survive the loss of a critical function, and it must inform investment in resilience controls
- Interdependencies between systems (e.g., HNetPay depending on network services) can create cascading failures that are not apparent from looking at individual systems in isolation
- A BIA is a living document that must be reviewed and updated whenever there are significant changes to the organization's systems, processes, or operating environment

## Relevant Frameworks
- NIST SP 800-34 Rev. 1 — Contingency Planning Guide for Federal Information Systems
- NIST CSF — Recover (RC) function: Recovery Planning and Improvements
- ISO 22301:2019 — Business Continuity Management Systems
- HIPAA Security Rule — Contingency Plan standard (45 CFR § 164.308(a)(7))
