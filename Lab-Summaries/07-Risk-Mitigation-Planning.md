# Lab 7: Risk Mitigation Planning

## Overview
Developed a risk mitigation plan for the prioritized risks identified during the risk assessment phase. Selected appropriate security controls, evaluated cost-benefit trade-offs, and defined implementation timelines and success metrics. Mapped selected controls to NIST CSF and ISO 27001 control categories.

## Skills Demonstrated
- Selecting risk treatment options: Accept, Avoid, Transfer, Mitigate
- Mapping security controls to risk register entries
- Performing cost-benefit analysis for proposed controls
- Defining measurable success criteria and implementation milestones for each mitigation

## Tools Used
- SimpleRisk (risk treatment and mitigation tracking)
- NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 security controls catalogue
- Cost-benefit analysis templates
- Control mapping matrices (NIST CSF ↔ ISO 27001 ↔ PCI DSS)

## Key Takeaways
- Risk treatment decisions must balance residual risk against the cost and feasibility of proposed controls
- Not all risks should be mitigated — risk acceptance is a valid strategy when residual risk falls within the organization's defined tolerance
- Security controls should address root causes, not just symptoms; compensating controls are acceptable interim measures with a documented remediation plan
- Control effectiveness must be validated through testing and monitoring after implementation

## Relevant Frameworks
- NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 — Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems and Organizations
- NIST CSF — Protect (PR) and Respond (RS) functions
- ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — Clause 6.1.3: Information security risk treatment
- PCI DSS Requirement 12 — Maintain an information security policy
