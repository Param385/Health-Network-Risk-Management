# Lab 4: Risk Assessment

## Overview
Performed a formal risk assessment for the healthcare organization's information systems using both qualitative and semi-quantitative approaches. Evaluated threats and vulnerabilities against critical assets, calculated inherent and residual risk levels using a risk matrix, and produced a prioritized list of risks requiring treatment.

## Skills Demonstrated
- Applying qualitative and semi-quantitative risk assessment methodologies
- Using a likelihood × impact risk matrix to calculate risk levels
- Differentiating between inherent risk and residual risk
- Producing a prioritized risk register from assessment findings

## Tools Used
- Risk matrix (5×5 likelihood/impact scale)
- SimpleRisk (risk documentation and scoring)
- NIST SP 800-30 threat and vulnerability catalogues 

## Key Takeaways
- Qualitative risk assessment enables rapid prioritization even without precise financial data
- Risk ratings must consider both the likelihood of a threat event and the potential impact on confidentiality, integrity, and availability
- Residual risk after controls are applied must still be compared against the organization's defined risk tolerance
- Consistent risk scoring criteria are essential for meaningful comparison across different systems and departments

## Relevant Frameworks
- NIST SP 800-30 Rev. 1 — Risk Assessment methodology and likelihood/impact scales
- NIST CSF — Identify (ID.RA): Risk Assessment
- ISO/IEC 27005:2022 — Information security risk management
- HIPAA Security Rule 45 CFR § 164.308(a)(1) — Risk Analysis requirement
